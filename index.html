Compass Quarterly Market Reports

Compass has released the fourth quarter 2019 market reports for Manhattan, Brooklyn and Queens, shedding light on both the end of the year and the decade. Perhaps this tidbit from the Manhattan report summarizes the market best:

Many saw [2019 performance] as evidence of an impending economic downturn. Our data paints a different picture, however. Even at the lowest point this year, prices stayed nearly 15% higher than ten years ago. This quarter, sales are up 1%, prices bounced back, inventory is lower, and mortgage rates are steady. While the future still holds economic uncertainty from shifts in trade, alliances, taxes, and more, it appears the real estate market in Manhattan is stabilizing. After the complicated start to 2019, buyers are wielding their finances confidently again and moving ahead, albeit cautiously. The increased average sales price and time on market support this.
 
There was a lot to crow about in Brooklyn, where new developments and the general expansion of the borough drove changes in price. The average price across Brooklyn has continued its upward trend, hitting the highest of any fourth quarter. Median price has done the same, reaching $800,000, the highest on record.

Real Estate Predictions for 2020
After looking back at the previous year, it's customary to make predictions about the year ahead. In 6sqft, real estate experts sound off on what to expect in 2020, including the long-term impact of rent regulations, sustainability and an enduring buyer's market.
 
Meanwhile, Curbed looks at how our NYC landscape might change in the coming year. Think megaprojects, transit and parks.

What's In and Out for Design This Year?
Keeping interiors fresh and au courant is especially important if you're considering selling your home this year. Elle Decor has once again collected the latest trends. What's in? A mix of old and new, whether that's traditional styles with a modern twist or a curated collection of high-low finds. What's out? Gray on gray on gray.
 
Over at Architectural Digest, designers are anticipating blockier furniture, statement fixtures and texture.

2020 Travel Trends
If you're ready to plan an epic adventure in the new year, look no further than Harper's Bazaar's 20 Best Places to Travel in 2020 — a jawdropping collection of (mostly) off-the-beaten-path locales.
 
Architectural Digest has compiled a wonderful list of the world's design capitals, and with sustainability very much on everyone's minds, AD has also assembled a list of sustainable design-centric destinations.

New Year, New Cybersecurity Threats
January is a great time to review your online security, update passwords and so on. As home assistant devices become more popular — maybe you received a Google Home or Amazon Echo device during the holidays — their impact on privacy becomes more concerning. The New York Post offers a solid rundown of what to know about in-home assistants and other devices, such as connected TVs, appliances, security systems and toys.
